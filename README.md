Thyrsus — named for the staff of Dionysus, the pine cone on a fennel stalk that the satyrs carried into the revels.
The accent is pine gold, bleeding into vine purple and wine red as frenzy builds. The cursor is a goat hoof print. Atmosphere particles are pine gold, vine purple, and wine crimson — they speed up and multiply as the frenzy meter rises.

The lute is a fully hand-drawn SVG oud-style instrument. Deep pear body with visible bowl stave ribs, a warm spruce belly with a central rosette — the rosette has a six-pointed star of Dionysus with a grape cluster in the center. Angled-back pegbox with 8 carved wooden pegs. A satyr face is carved into the pegbox — little horns, lapis eyes, a grinning mouth, a wispy beard. Ivy leaves scattered on the body edges. Vine inlay along the rim. Tailpiece and strap button at the bottom.
Click any string directly. Eight strings fan from nut to bridge, thickness graduating bass to treble, warm gold to silver-bright.

Five authentic Greek modes — each attributed to a mythological figure with a line of poetry:

Dorian — "Pan dances at the crossroads" (the noble mode, strong)
Phrygian — "Dionysus descends from Thrace, wild-eyed and laughing" (the dark one — genuinely unsettling half-step opening)
Lydian — "Apollo lifts the lyre, the Muses turn to listen" (bright raised 4th)
Mixolydian — "Silenus stumbles through the vines, singing anyway" (flat 7th, that bluesy droop)
Bacchic — "The maenads run through the dark pines" (invented — pentatonic with chromatic passing tones)

Three playing styles:

Pluck — clean gut string attack, natural harmonic decay
Strum — slightly slower attack, warmer
Wild — three detuned copies with pitch wobble LFOs, scratchy bow noise

The Frenzy Meter is the real innovation. Play fast and it builds. Slow down and it decays. As it rises the header label goes: calm → roused → ecstatic → MAENAD. The color of the waveform shifts from pine gold through vine purple to wine red. The ripple on pluck changes color. At high frenzy, every note gets an inharmonic buzzing overtone — slightly off the third harmonic, the dissonance of a man who has been dancing for six hours. The atmosphere particles accelerate.
Six Bacchic Riffs based on Greek dithyrambic poetry rhythms:

Ascend / Dance (dactylic foot: long-short-short) / Invocation (slow, ceremonial, calling Dionysus) / Revel (dense overlapping random bursts) / Frenzy (24 rapid-fire notes, chaos) / Lament (slow descend, the morning after)

The Resin knob adds the body resonance peak of the wood — each string has a slightly different resonant frequency across the body. Turn it up and you feel the instrument.
